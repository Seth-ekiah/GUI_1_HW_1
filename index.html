Welcome to Jupiter!

If you stand outside on a clear night and look up, you might just be able to spot the planet Jupiter, as it is the third brightest object in the night sky. Jupiter is so bright because it is the largest planet in our solar system, having a diameter of eleven Earth's! Next time you are out star gazing, see if you can spot Jupiter!

What is Jupiter Like?

Jupiter is a gas giant, meaning that it is all clouds and has no surface. These clouds, made up mostly of hydrogen and helium, are noticeably turbulent, with large storms raging all over the planet. Most notably, the Great Red Spot is Jupiter's largest storm and has been raging since at least 1831! You can see the storm in most images of Jupiter. Jupiter also has an extremely large magnetic field. So large, in fact, that it almost reaches Saturn's orbit! This magnetic field is very strong, and causes an aurorae to appear around its north and south poles. This is why Jupiter appears with blue rings around the top and bottom in some images.

Meet the Galilean Moons!

Scientists have actually observed that Jupiter has at least ninety-seven moons! The four largest moons, however, are known as the Galilean Moons, because they were discovered by Galileo himself. However, it was Johannes Kepler who decided to name the mooons:

Ganymede

Callisto

Io

Europa

How did Jupiter get its Name?

When the ancient Romans began to observe Jupiter, they named it after their god of the sky and thunder - Jupiter. To the ancient Greeks this god was also known by a more familiar name - Zeus.
